Coordinate and use company resources to provide value added services to accounts.

 

Provide analysis and information on sales forecast and budget preparation

Job Title: Apprentice Operator

Department: Plant

Reports To: Supervisor and Plant Manager

FLSA Status: Non-Exempt

Shift: Days 5:00am-5:30pm, Rotating 12s

Salary: $14-$18/hour

 

Essential Duties and Responsibilities include the following. Other duties may be assigned.

 

Responsible for all activities associated with running a Flexo or Rotary Die Cutter including: setup, operation, cleanup, and recommending improvements to operations.

 

Sets up and runs the Load Former pre-feeder, feeding corrugated sheets through the machine

 

Reviews job tickets, print cards, and quality inspection paperwork with machine Captain

 

Reads job order and mounts specified  rubber print plate and printing dies on cylinder.

 

Installs cylinder on shaft, and secures assembly in bed of press.

 

Fills dye fonts with prescribed inks or dyes.

 

Moves levers to engage dye rolls, adjusting position of cylinder to print two or more colors without overlapping or smearing previously printed area.

 

Starts press, adjusting speed, tension, and alignment of printing plate as necessary.

 

Cuts sample of printed material from end of printed roll and inspects for imperfections, such as blurred or off-center impressions.

 

Cleans rubber plate, cylinder, and dye fonts, using solvent, and lubricates press as needed, using grease gun.

 

May mix inks or dyes to produce desired colors.

 

Sets up rails, print, and die cut section on machinery accurately in accordance with proper setup techniques.

 

Inspects all dies before and after run to ensure that any necessary repairs are made before the next run.

 

Ensures all bolts for cutting dies are in place and tightly secured.

 

Monitor box quality, including, but not limited to, loose liner, warp, correct size, and print.

 

Checks anilox rolls for damage.

 

Maximizes the utilization of equipment by operating the machinery at available capacity and speed.

Cross trains and assist Helpers, Stackers, and Bundlers with job duties on the machine.

 

Completes necessary reports in an accurate and timely manner, including but not limited to: Production Data,

Machine Operational Status, Safety Data, Quality Data. Turn in maintenance work orders to supervision for needed repairs.

 

Adherence to company policies, including its attendance is essential.

Job Title: Maintenance Technician

Department: Maintenance

Reports To: Maintenance Manager, Indirect report to Operations

FLSA Status: Non-Exempt, Hourly

Shift: Nights, Rotating 12s

Salary: $20/hr starting; negotiable with experience

 

Summary Repairs and maintains production machinery, conveyor lines  mechanical and hydraulic components of production machines and equipment such as fabricating machine tools, material handling system, and automated lubrication system by performing the following duties.

 

Essential Duties and Responsibilities include the following. Other duties may be assigned.

 

Visually inspects and listens to machines and equipment to locate causes of malfunctions while working with machine operators.

 

Ensures safe operation of machinery, mechanical, electrical, pneumatic systems equipment and components.

 

Observes all health and safety requirements and maintains work area in neat order and orderly condition.

 

Uses common tools including but not limited to: hand and power tools such as hammers, saws, drills and wrenches, precision measuring instruments, and electrical and electronic testing devices.

 

Troubleshoots sources of equipment problems by observing mechanical, electrical, pneumatic systems equipment and component devices in the operation; uses precision measuring and testing instruments. Knowledge of 3 –phase electrical, motor control center, pump, motors, compressors, variable frequency drives, wiring and PLC Programming required.

 

Inspects motors and belts, checks fluid levels, replaces filters, and performs other maintenance actions while following maintenance procedures.

 

Cleans and lubricates shafts, bearings, gears, and other parts of equipment per work procedures or technical manual.

 

Perform  preventative maintenance and predictive maintenance of process equipment throughout the plant  to include mechanical, pneumatic, valves, lines, connections, pumps, and electrical maintenance.

 

Reports immediately to supervisor problems and unusual situations which cannot be resolved quickly.

 

Performs upgrades and install new systems when required.

 

Repairs broken parts. Sets up and operates metalworking tools such as lathe, drill press, or grinder, to make or repair parts.

 

Operates forklifts and high lifts, and other powered lift equipment as needed

 

Adherence to company policies, including its attendance is essential. Must be flexible to come in early, stay late and may need to be “on call" in case of emergencies.
