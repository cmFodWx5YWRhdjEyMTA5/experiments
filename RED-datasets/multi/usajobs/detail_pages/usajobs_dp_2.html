Job Overview

Summary

About the Agency

This position is located in the Information and Applications Division of the Office of the Chief Information Officer (OCIO). The incumbent applies principals of data analysis, computer science, computational methods, mathematics and numerical analysis to develop solutions for aerospace, aeronautics, space communication, structures and materials, and institutional customers.

The work involves analyzing and understanding complex problems in these and other disciplines and developing the most efficient and feasible solutions to address them. The incumbent leads a team of data scientists, application developers, and solution architects, providing technical and administrative leadership in the application of data science, data analytics, and visualization to aerospace and aeronautics data. The incumbent is responsible for evaluating and recommending hardware, software, and software services for processing, reducing, analyzing, and visualizing data.

As identical vacancies are identified, additional selections may be made from this announcement.

To receive consideration, you must submit a resume and answer NASA-specific questions. The NASA questions appear after you submit your resume and are transferred to the NASA web site. If you successfully apply, USAJOBS will show your application status as 'Received'. If your status is 'Application Status Not Available', you have not successfully applied. Do not rely on a USAJOBS email to confirm successful application. Only an email from NASA confirms a successful application.

Duties

Performs requirements analysis, and plans development and implementation to ensure that project scope and requirements are defined and documented, projects are formally approved, and milestones and lower level reviews are accomplished. Manages and controls computer science and information technology projects throughout their lifecycle. Creates appropriate project plan elements including scope definition, resource requirements, budget analysis, procurement plan, communications plan, risk management plan, and IT security compliance plan. Defines roles and responsibilities for project team members and identifies work packages, milestones, and deliverables.

Makes technical project decisions, and analyzes and consolidates project approaches and resource requirements. Establishes methods and procedures to substantially reduce project costs. As a recognized expert authority in the fields related to computational sciences, provides expert guidance to management for strategic planning and program development.

Conducts conceptual definition analyses and establishes preliminary applications and alternatives for a variety of advanced systems on proposed new projects for which no precedents are available. Originates and establishes unique methods and applications that provide innovative solutions to complex computing issues. Develops original concepts for uses in advanced technologies, systems, system software, automation, and information processing. Identifies alternative approaches or compromises to develop the most cost effective technical solutions. Ensures compatibility with overall objectives and schedules. Integrates various research and analysis results into comprehensive technical briefings, reports, and technical papers. Provides technical advice in area of specialization both within and outside the organization. Performs additional data systems and analysis work.

Conducts or directs studies of aerospace, aeronautical and/or science problems, analyzes computer models, and identifies solutions. Develops and applies computer simulation techniques. Defines problems or projects, resolves critical problems, or identifies problems requiring new or novel approaches. Develops new or novel algorithms, codes, mathematical solutions, or computer system solutions when standard methods or practices are not sufficient. Keeps abreast of current developments in information technology, computer science, visualization, mathematical science, and related technology fields. Conducts site visits to assess the application of the latest state-of-the-art technology and their applicability to the requirements of the agency.

Provides mentoring, guidance and technical advice to less experienced employees within the organization in the performance of related assignments and is available to those employees for consultation on assigned projects. Initiates contact and provides expert technical advice and direction to contractor professionals. Serves as a senior technical contact and/or Contracting Officer's Representative. Keeps the Contracting Officer informed on progress, proposed contract modifications, validity of claims, analysis of proposals, and assessment of contract time extensions.

Qualifications

Applicant must have one (1) year (52 weeks) of specialized experience equivalent to the GS-13 grade level, which has equipped the applicant with the particular competencies needed to successfully perform the duties of the position described above.

For this announcement, specialized experience is defined as:

- Demonstrated experience which includes providing technical leadership, advice, consulting, and assistance on complex, controversial or precedent-setting matters and dealing tactfully and effectively with engineers, scientists, and senior leadership.

- Demonstrated experience successfully advocating for significant resources, and establishing and implementing a technical vision to improve mission capabilities.

- Demonstrated experience working with technologies for scientific computing analysis such as scientific visualization hardware, software and infrastructure.

- Demonstrated experience performing hands-on data analytics using modern tools and techniques in machine learning, data mining, and algorithm development.

Basic Education Requirement: A bachelor's degree from an accredited college or university with major study in engineering, physical science, mathematics, life sciences, computer science, or other field of science. Degrees in engineering technology are not considered to be qualifying for this position.

U.S. citizenship is required.

Agency Employees Only: This opportunity is open to Glenn Research Center Federal employees currently serving on one of the following appointment types: career, career-conditional, VRA, Schedule A appointment for individuals with disabilities, or NASA term appointment that provides eligibility to convert to a career or career conditional appointment. NASA employees on long-term rotational assignments to the NASA Engineering and Safety Center or the NASA Safety Center may also apply.

How to Apply

This vacancy is being filled through NASA STARS, an automated Staffing and Recruitment System. NASA partners with USAJOBS in providing a seamless application process. Before you begin the application process, please read the vacancy announcement carefully and have all required information available. You may begin the process of submitting your resume by clicking on the 'Apply Online' link. In order to be considered, you must submit a resume completed on the USAJOBS site. When completing your USAJOBS resume, please remember that NASA limits resumes to approximately 32,000 characters including spaces. NASA does not accept resumes uploaded to USAJOBS from a second source or documents attached through USAJOBS' document attachment feature. Once you submit your resume to NASA, you will be asked to complete a short series of additional questions. You must finish the entire process in order to have a complete application package and receive consideration. Your answers will not be saved unless you finish the entire application. You may edit a previously-submitted application, if the announcement is still open. If you are unable to apply electronically for this position, submit your resume and supplemental questions to:

How You Will Be Evaluated

Resumes will be rated by an automated system (Resumix) that matches the competencies extracted from the candidate's resume to the competencies identified by the selecting official for the position. Candidates will be evaluated on the competencies they possess that are directly related to the duties of the job, as described in the announcement. Candidates should refer to NASA's Applicant Guide for assistance in developing a complete resume, as NASA will not accept separate KSA statements.

Qualified candidates will be assigned to one of three quality levels based on the degree to which their competencies meet the duties required. A human resources specialist will validate the qualifications of those candidates eligible to be referred to the selecting official. For the purpose of the Career Transition Assistance Program (CTAP), candidates rated in the top quality level are considered well-qualified.

Required Documents

NASA's application process has been specifically developed to ensure that we only ask you for the information we absolutely need to evaluate your qualifications and eligibility. In order to apply for this position, you only need to submit your resume and answer the screening questions and supplemental information. No additional documentation is accepted at the time of application. (For example you need not submit narrative 'KSA' statements; they are not required and will not be evaluated.) If you are claiming veterans' preference, we may ask you to submit proof of veterans preference (DD-214, and, if claiming 10-point preference, SF-15 plus proof required by that form). If you fail to provide the required documents within the stated time period, we may withdraw a job offer and/or remove you from further consideration.
