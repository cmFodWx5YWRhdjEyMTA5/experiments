Job Overview

Summary

About the Agency

The selectee for this position will serve as the Assistant Program Manager for T&E (APMT&E) in the Systems Test & Evaluation Management Division of the Air 1.0 Programs Test Branch of NAVAIRWARCENAC DIV.

Duties

The successful selectee will perform the following duties:

  • Responsible to the Program Manager and Deputy Program Manager (PM/DPM) in the overarching execution of all developmental and operational testing.
  • Subject matter expert on all test and evaluation matters and will represent the PM/DPM in meetings with DOT&E, CJCS, U.S. Army, U.S. Air Force, OPNAV and Operational Test Forces.
  • Knowledgeable of all NAVAIR T&E policies and provides guidance consistent with the PM's acquisition strategy and Capabilities/ Requirements Documents.
  • Chairs Test and Evaluation (T&E) Working Integrated Product Team meetings, be responsible for Test and Evaluation Master Plan configuration management and currency, planning, coordination and execution of Operational Test Readiness Reviews and acts on behalf of the PM/DPM as the liaison between all T&E program stakeholders, decision and policy makers.
  • Maintains the program office master test schedule and T&E metrics and is responsible for integrating test issues across the Integrated Program Teams.

Qualifications

In order to qualify for this position, your resume must provide sufficient experience and/or education, knowledge, skills, and abilities to perform the duties of the specific position for which you are being considered. Your resume is the key means we have for evaluating your skills, knowledge, and abilities as they relate to this position. Therefore, we encourage you to be clear and specific when describing your experience.

Applicants must meet the following positive education qualifications requirements of the Office of Personnel Management (OPM) Qualifications Standards Manual: Applicants must possess an engineering, physics, operations research, or computer science (or related) degree from an accredited university.

In addition to the Basic Requirements for this position, your resume must also demonstrate at least one year of specialized experience at or equivalent to the DP-04 (GS-12/13) grade level or pay band in the Federal service or equivalent experience in the private or public sector. Specialized experience must demonstrate the following: 1) apply Naval Flight Testing principles and practices sufficient to properly represent Test & Evaluation organization with all Test & Evaluation program stakeholders, decision and policy makers; 2) provide expertise of Test & Evaluation policies and instructions to advise senior leadership on Test & Evaluation subjects, and application of policies and instructions to an acquisition strategy and documents such as Capabilities Documents and Test & Evaluation Master Plan (TEMP); and 3) maintain program office master test schedule and metrics to coordinate and monitor test issues across the Integrated Product Teams (IPTs) or organization.

Additional qualification information can be found from the following Office of Personnel Management website:
https://www.opm.gov/policy-data-oversight/classification-qualifications/general-schedule-qualification-standards/#url=GS-PROF.

You will receive credit for all qualifying experience, including volunteer and part time experience. You must clearly identify the duties and responsibilities in each position held and the total number of hours per week.

Experience refers to paid and unpaid experience, including volunteer work done through National Service programs (e.g., professional, philanthropic, religious, spiritual, community, student, social). Volunteer work helps build critical competencies, knowledge, and skills and can provide valuable training and experience that translates directly to paid employment.

This position is covered under the Defense Acquisition Workforce Improvement Act (DAWIA) and requires additional education, training and experience. This position has been identified as a Career Field T - Test & Evaluation at Level 3. If you possess DAWIA Certification, please indicate your Certification Level and Career Field information in your resume.

Applicants not certified may still apply and be selected, but must achieve certification within 24 months of appointment. Certification requirements may be viewed at http://icatalog.dau.mil/onlinecatalog/CareerLvl.aspx.

This position is a designated as Critical Acquisition Positions (CAPs). In addition to DAWIA certification requirements, individuals selected for CAP must also be a member of the Acquisition Corps, become a member, or obtain a position waiver at the time of selection and sign a three-year tenure agreement prior to assuming the position. If you are an Acquisition Corps member, please indicate your membership in your resume. Acquisition Corps requirements may be viewed at http://www.secnav.navy.mil/rda/workforce/Documents/dawiaoperatingguide21dec2011forwebsite1.pdf
