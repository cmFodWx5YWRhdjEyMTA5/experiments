Job Overview

Summary

Civilian employees serve a vital role in supporting the Army mission. They provide the skills that are not readily available in the military, but crucial to support military operations. The Army integrates the talents and skills of its military and civilian members to form a Total Army.

About the Position:

Scott AFB is located near the city of Belleville, IL, in St. Clair County, approximately 30 minutes from St. Louis, MO. All branches of services are assigned to the base. Transportation includes Metro Link Light Rail which runs from the St. Louis metropolitan area to Scott AFB, and convenient interstate and highways. MidAmerica St. Louis and Lambert-St. Louis International Airports are nearby. St. Clair County is surrounded by several major tourist attractions.

Duties

As a Computer Scientist, you will be responsible for the following:

  • Serves as a Computer Scientist, Information Technology Branch, Systems Integration Division (SI), Surface Deployment and Distribution Command Transportation Engineering Agency (SDDCTEA), responsible for research and development, design, acquisition, and testing of computer based transportation modeling, simulation, automation tool and related systems.
  • Technical advisor for modeling and simulation systems to the Chief, Information Technology Branch.
  • Assignments involve (a) research into computer hardware and software systems with potential modeling and simulation capabilities; (b) development of new or substantially improved modeling and simulation computer capabilities.
  • Performs overall system analysis and integration of complex and diverse transportation modeling, simulation and analysis tools.
  • Serves as a specialist in preliminary design and analytical design functions for transportation modeling and simulation systems.
  • Receive new and different transportation automation requirements, determines basic approach, proposes hardware and software system solutions (ensuring specifications are met), procures and implements solution.
  • Plans, organizes, and conducts systematic, critical, and thorough research in data processing equipment and applications. This effort will identify computer hardware and software with potential transportation modeling and simulation capabilities.
  • Recommends procedures to accommodate various transportation models, simulations and analysis tools.
  • Implement transportation modeling, simulation and analysis tools on automated data processing (ADP) equipment using bold, new procedures to accommodate the ever-expanding automation requirements of the transportation models and tools.

Job Requirements

Key Requirements

  • This position requires IAT Level II certification within 6 months.

Qualifications

In order to qualify, you must meet the education and/or experience requirements described below.  Your resume must clearly describe your relevant experience; if qualifying based on education, your transcripts will be required as part of your application.  Additional information about transcripts is in this document.

Experience required: To qualify based on your work experience, your resume must describe at least one year of experience equivalent to the next lower grade/level GS-12, which prepared you to do the work in this job.  Specialized experience is defined as: (1) applying computer science principles in the development, maintenance and administration of transportation modeling, simulation and geographic information systems (GIS) applications; (2) implementing and maintaining high-performance servers to include virtual server environments; (3) interpreting and applying Security Technical Implementation Guides (STIG) and applying Information Assurance (IA) principles; (4) producing Information Technology (IT) system design solutions for complex problems, incorporating data management design, web architecture, and client/server architectures.

You will be evaluated on the basis of your level of competency (knowledge, skills, abilities) in the following areas:

  • Computer Science Concepts and Information Systems Management
  • Information Technology System Design, Standards, and Information Assurance
  • Communication

Time in Grade Requirement:  Applicants who have held a General Schedule (GS) position within the last 52 weeks must have 52 weeks of Federal service at the GS-12 grade (or equivalent).

Education - Basic Requirement:

Bachelor's degree in computer science or bachelor's degree with 30 semester hours in a combination of mathematics, statistics, and computer science. At least 15 of the 30 semester hours must have included any combination of statistics and mathematics that included differential and integral calculus. All academic degrees and course work must be from accredited or pre-accredited institutions.
