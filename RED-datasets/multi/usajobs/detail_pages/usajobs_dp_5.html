Job Overview

Summary

Civilian employees serve a vital role in supporting the Army mission. They provide the skills that are not readily available in the military, but crucial to support military operations. The Army integrates the talents and skills of its military and civilian members to form a Total Army.

About the Position:
The U.S. Army Research Laboratory (ARL) is participating in an alternative personnel system known as the Laboratory Personnel Demonstration Project (DEMO). Among other features, the Demonstration Project replaced GS grade levels with occupational families and pay bands. The pay band DB-04 is equal to the former GS-14 step 01 through GS-15 step 10.
This position is located in Adelphi, MD which is in Prince George's County, a metropolitan area, bordered to the south by Washington, DC and to the north by Baltimore. It is approximately 30 miles south of Baltimore and the White Marsh Area, and 10 miles north of Washington, DC.
The Research Laboratory is recognized as a frontier for Army research programs with substantial affect on national defense issues and new technology areas. To learn more about the Army Research Laboratory you can check their website at http://www.arl.army.mil.

Duties

The selected candidate will serve as the Social Computing Basic Research Team Leader, responsible for leading a multidisciplinary team of computer scientists, computational linguists, cognitive psychologists, and information scientists examining fundamental issues in the use of social media for data analytics. The candidate must have a proven track record, as demonstrated by previous experience collaborating on basic research projects, successful publication of peer-reviewed technical papers and journal articles, invited presentations at major conferences, and/or significant contributions as a technical reviewer, panelist, or organizer of research working groups. Specialized knowledge for this position includes a background in natural language processing and its application to large scale social media and social network analysis in support of situational understanding and decision-making. Responsibilities also include: 1) establishing near and long-term team objectives based on priorities and resources jointly established with management; (2) allocating labor of the research team across mission and customer-funded projects; and (3) establishing, collaborating with, and conducting joint research efforts with academia, industry, and other government partners. The incumbent will review the progress of team members and provide input on employee performance to the supervising branch chief. The selected candidate must be capable of leading senior researchers and mentoring junior researchers, helping to develop the next generation of scientists and engineers to support the US Army.

Qualifications

Qualified candidates must meet the Basic Requirement for the Psychologist (0180) or Computer Scientist (1550). In addition to meeting the Basic Requirement, applicants must also have one year of specialized experience at the DB-03 (GS-13 equivalent) level. There is no substitution of education for experience at this level.

Basic Qualifications for DB-0180 Psychologist:
Bachelor's Degree in Psychology or in an equivalent major

Basic Requirements for DB-1550-04 Computer Scientist:
Bachelor's degree in computer science.
OR
Bachelor's degree with 30 semester hours in a combination of mathematics, statistics, and computer science. 15 of my 30 semester hours included any combination of statistics and mathematics that included differential and integral calculus. My academic degree and course work was from accredited or pre-accredited institutions.

SPECIALIZED EXPERIENCE: To qualify based on your experience, your resume must describe at least one year of experience which prepared you to do the work in this job. I have one year of specialized experience equivalent to the next lower grade/pay band DB-03(GS-13) in the Federal Service which includes: 1) knowledge of natural language processing and its applications; 2) leveraging social media and conducting social network analysis; and 3) leading a research group of multidisciplinary engineers and scientists.

You will be evaluated on the basis of your level of competency in the following areas:
