4 Bed Family Hse With Stunning 20ft KitchenB'fast | 2 4  
 
Property Description
Imaginatively extended family home with stunning 20ft 'open plan' kitchen/breakfast Room, incorporating solid oak handmade units together with granite work surfaces and a 'wrap around' double-glazed roof. Further careful attention has been given to both retaining the character features of the property, (including replacement timber double glazed sash windows) but also stylish contemporary additions including bi-fold shutters to the main windows. Enclosed west facing rear garden. Helen Road offers convenient access to the railway station & the city centre beyond.


West Oxford is some 15 minutes' walk west of the heart of Oxford city and a few minutes from the busy intercity rail station. This area is located amongst several green spaces and canal/riverside walks. 'Modern' West Oxford, was built on the site of Osney's ancient Abbey, and now extends west across from the cottages of Osney Island. These short streets comprise of both Victorian, Edwardian and 1930s houses, all close to the open parks/green areas. The community supports a good range of shops, several public houses, an excellent primary school, local businesses and community centre. Waitrose has announced plans to provide a new food store on Botley Road, and they are considering views before submitting a planning application, this is on the former MFI/Halfords site. West Oxford has an active and supportive community

The houses in Helen Road were first laid out in 1902 by Thomas Henry Kingerlee. In the 'The Changing Faces Of West Oxford' by Malcolm Graham, he makes mention that Thomas Henry Kingerlee named both Henry Road and Helen Road after two of his children. Number 5 Helen Road is a stunning home which has benefited from meticulous attention to details by the present owners and inspection is recommended.
