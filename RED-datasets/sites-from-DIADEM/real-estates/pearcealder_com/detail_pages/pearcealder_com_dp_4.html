Property Description
A Charming Two Bedroom Victorian House With Period Features, Extended Ground Floor Accommodation, Forming An 18ft Kitchen/Dining Room With Twin Doors & Side Windows Leading Out To The Enclosed Rear Garden. Osney Island Is A Popular Conservation Area Located Just To The West Of The City Centre That Affords Convenient Access To The Railway Station With The City Centre Beyond. No Onward Chain.

Osney Island is a popular residential locality with a supportive community and residents association, situated just to the west of central Oxford, and for the main part comprising of period brick and stone terraced properties. Now a conservation area, it gets its name because it is surrounded by the River Thames & tributaries, and approached from the Botley Road by way of a road bridge. The island/west Oxford is some 15 minutes' walk west of the heart of Oxford city and a few minutes from the busy intercity rail station. This area is located amongst several green spaces and canal/riverside walks. 'Modern' West Oxford, was built on the site of Osney's ancient Abbey, and now extends west across from the cottages of Osney Island. These short streets comprise of both Victorian, Edwardian and 1930s houses, all close to the open parks/green areas. The community supports a good range of shops, several public houses, an excellent primary school, local businesses and community centre. Waitrose has announced plans to provide a new food store on Botley Road, and the planning application has been passed by Oxford City Council, this new store is to be located on the former MFI/Halfords site.
