Helen Road, Oxford Sold | POA
 
 
Three Bedroom Property For Updating | 1 3  
 
Property Description
Mature Three Bedroom Terrace House With Rear Gardens Backing Onto A Tributary Of The Thames & Enjoying Open Views Over To Botley Park. The Property Is The Penultimate House At The End Of the Street, With Helen Road (A 'No Thru Road') Affording Direct Pedestrian Access Into Botley Park. This Property Has Been A Home To The Present Owners Since 1951 & Offers The Opportunity For Updating And Modernisation.

West Oxford is some 15 minutes' walk west of the heart of Oxford city and a few minutes from the busy intercity rail station. This area is located amongst several green spaces and canal/riverside walks. 'Modern' West Oxford, was built on the site of Osney's ancient Abbey, and now extends west across from the cottages of Osney Island. These short streets comprise of both Victorian, Edwardian and 1930s houses, all close to the open parks/green areas. The community supports a good range of shops, several public houses, an excellent primary school, local businesses and community centre.

The houses in Helen Road were first laid out in 1902 by Thomas Henry Kingerlee. In the 'The Changing Faces Of West Oxford' by Malcolm Graham, he makes mentioned that Thomas Henry Kingerlee named both Henry Road and Helen Road after two of his children. Due to the positioning of the property, number 56 is wider than many of the other houses within the street. The rear garden backs onto a tributary of the Thames, with open views out to Botley Park beyond.
