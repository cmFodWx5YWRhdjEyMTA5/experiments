Charming Home With Views Over To Thames Tributary | 1 1  
 
Property Description
A Charming Victorian End of Terrace Home Delightfully Positioned, With Unrestricted Views Over A Thames Tributary & Out To St. Frideswides Church. The Property Offers A Stylish Interior With A Tasteful Blend Of Character Features & Contemporary Fittings. Osney Island Is A Popular Conservation Area With A Supportive Community, Positioned West Of The City Centre Affording Convenient Access To The Railway Station.

Osney Island is a popular residential locality with a supportive community and residents association, situated to west of central Oxford and for the main part comprising of period brick and stone terraced properties. Now a conservation area, it gets its name because it is surrounded by the River Thames & tributaries, and approached from the Botley Road, by way of a road bridge. The island offers access to the mainline railway station with the city center beyond. There are useful facilities nearby, including a community centre, primary school, parks and local shops.

The property is currently arranged as a one bedroom house with adjoining dressing room, however it is thought with some remodeling, including the removal of the wrought iron stairs case within the dressing room, as well as the installation of a landing wall, the property can be re-instated back as a two bedroom home (subject to regs). Currently the accommodation comprises of hallway, two receptions room with arch in-between, kitchen breakfast room with fitted appliances and twin doors to the garden, on the ground floor. Stairs rise from the hallway to a small landing, with doors to both the bathroom and dressing room which in-turn opens to the bedroom, and the dressing room with wrought iron stairs to a boarded loft.
