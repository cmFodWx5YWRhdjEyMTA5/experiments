    A writers retreat - be inspired in this idyllic setting, or simply enjoy the sunset and watching dolphins play in your uninterupted seascape. Snowdonia National Park at its best, this is the perfect place to seriously relax. Only 4.5 hours from London and 2.5 hours from Birmingham, this is a great holiday cottage for those really wanting to recharge from the stresses of modern life. A 3 bedroom detached house with balcony and stone-faced elevations, loved dearly by the current owner but now ready for you to make your mark. Set in semi-wild hillside with a small orchard, pond, vegtable garden, flowers, shrubs and lawn. There is parking for several cars and sundry outhouses. Ideal for those with an interest in painting, photography, birdwatching, wild flowers, sailing, fishing, beaches, surfing, cycling, hill walking, golf, castles and dolphins. Llangelynin is an unspoilt hamlet with 13th Century Church, six miles north of the seaside town Tywyn, the Church is one of the oldest and remotest in Wales. The local beaches are beautiful with fabulous dunes and said to be some of the best in Britain, the surfing is pretty good locally. The worlds first preserved railway at Talyllyn is a major attraction locally. The Dolgoch waterfalls are also close by. There is a station nearby at Tonfanau (2.8 miles) you can catch a train from Euston to Tonfanau which takes just over 4.5 hours, a taxi to Llangelynin will cost £10, so getting to this remote idyll really couldnt be easier. Tywyn has a range of shopping, pubs and cafes.
