An elegant Cotswold house set in private and beautifully landscaped gardens. Milton House has over four thousand square feet of light and spacious accommodation, renovated to a high standard with luxurious fittings throughout. The light is a result of an abundance of doors and windows, pale travertine marble and light oak floors. With 4/5 reception rooms, 5/6 bedrooms and 4 bathrooms, the accommodation lends itself equally to family living and to entertaining as one room flows easily into the next. The triple aspect drawing room opens with double doors into the orangery, allowing views to the garden from both rooms. In fact every room in the house boasts a wonderful view of gardens or fields adding to the feeling of spaciousness and tranquillity. The accommodation is arranged over two floors: on the ground floor there are 4 reception rooms, kitchen breakfast room with woodburner, additional living/bedroom with ensuite shower room, two cloakrooms, a utility room and a boot room. On the main first floor there is a principle suite of bedroom, bathroom, dressing room and den area, three further double bedrooms, a family bathroom and a shower room. There is a further large study/bedroom which is approached by a separate staircase. With five sets of French doors, the house is cool in the summer and with excellent central heating and gas designer fires in the drawing and sitting room, Milton House is a delight to live in during the winter months. Location. Nestling between the Church and The Manor House in the historic hamlet of Yelford and overlooking fields and gardens, Milton House is the perfect family home. Yelford is wonderfully peaceful, surrounded by trees and farmland and the birdsong is incredible. Bridle paths and rights of way across the fields provide a wonderful variety of walks, right on your doorstep. Although the houses within the hamlet are spread apart from each other, the community spirit is high so there is never a feeling of isolation. Yelford is 12 miles from Oxford, with fast train and bus connections to London, and 4 miles from Witney, a vibrant town on the edge of the Cotswolds. There you will find a good range of boutiques and high street shops including Waitrose, Sainsburys and Marks and Spencers, restaurants, pubs, cinema complex and sports centre. Schools in the area are highly regarded in both state and independent sectors, with Cokethorpe school only minutes away. In less than thirty minutes by car, you will reach Oxford and its colleges, a city steeped in history, or Burford, a picturesque country town full of antique shops and a wonderful garden centre. Slightly further afield takes you to the regency town of Cheltenham with its famous race course and music and literary festivals or Stow on the Wold, an historic market town, close to a number of famous gardens within the hills and dales of the Cotswolds.
