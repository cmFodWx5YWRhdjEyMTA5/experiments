    Think artisan feel and period features combined with modern fresh interiors and clean lines – me thinks an architect may have touched this house with their magic.

    What its Got

    Walk through the door and if there isn’t a smile on your face before we finish the ground floor I’ll be surprised. This house feels like no other 1930’s house I have visited to date, the feeling of space is incredible, mix that with the solid build of the era; this is a great house to buy. There is a separate study to the front of the house, a large entrance hall and loo. The real selling point here is the kitchen dining room and sitting room that lead onto the garden. The kitchen is modern and fresh and open to the dining area which has an entire wall of library shelving. Steps lead down into a large sitting room with a wall of windows and doors leading to the garden; making this the perfect summer room, there is also a wood burner to keep you toasty in the winter months. The garden is about 100ft long and has a delightful range of shrubs and trees. On the first floor there are 3 bedrooms and a family bathroom.

    Where it is

    Cricket Road is in vibrant East Oxford which is a mile from the City Centre. On your door step there is a profusion of restaurants and shops that satisfy a vast slice of the community. From café culture and a hearty fry at Coco’s while you catch up with friends on a leisurely Sunday morning to good home cooked polish comfort food there is something for most people here. Oxford's never been short of cool kids with guitars and designs on the perfect pop song; if you want to meet them there’ll probably be at the Zodiac. If you want something a little more relaxing there are some wonderful concerts at the Jacqueline Du Pre Music Building.

    You would be hard pushed to go out in need of absolutely anything and not find it hidden in one of the interesting stores along the Cowley Road. For those of you who want to keep the excesses at bay there is the Roger Bannister sports ground, followed by a relaxing sports massage by Tom Bedford. The Botanic Gardens are a short walk, where better to contemplate the world than in a beautiful garden next to the river. All this and a bus into London every five minutes.
