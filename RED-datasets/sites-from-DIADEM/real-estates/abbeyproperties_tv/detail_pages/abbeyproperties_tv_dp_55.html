  • DESCRIPTION

    This impressive detached family home sits in grounds approaching c.3/4 and has panoramic countryside views to the rear over fields and countryside. The property offers well planned accommodation with an outstanding farm house kitchen-diner with an oil fired Rayburn as a focal point. The living room has a multi fuel wood burning stove in the fireplace and in turn opens up into the spacious Oak framed Sun room with vaulted ceiling and open views over the gardens and view to the rear. Also on the ground floor is the study with open fireplace and the useful utility room and cloakroom which again are set to the rear of the house. On the first floor the master bedroom has an en-suite bathroom with separate shower and 3 further double bedrooms and family bathroom. Other features in the property are the ceramic floors to the ground floor and the wooden flooring in the sun room which has underfloor heating. Outside, the property sits back from the road with a gated access to the gravel driveway which sweeps across the front of the house and back to the side and rear providing ample parking and access to the stable block. The gardens are broken up into different areas and there is an abundance of mature trees and shrubs and even a productive fruit & herb cage. One of the main features of the property is the openness of the view to the rear and with those who have an equestrian interest the property has a 4 bay stable block, however this is currently being used as a workshop and a games room. *(N.B nearby to the rear of the property is a commercial Livery which could provide rentable paddock land for horses but this would be by private negotiation and would not form any part of a sales contract).

  • LOCATION

    Curbridge is one of the closest villages to Witney town and therefore easily accessible. The slip road to the A40 which provides access to Oxford, Burford, Cheltenham and London can be reached in just a few minutes by car. Curbridge predominantly consists of period properties and mature houses with a limited number of modern houses and no large developments. It is surrounded by open countryside and the excellent shopping, education and leisure facilities of Witney. The bustling Market Town of Witney lies some 12 miles west of Oxford City and is well situated for access to the A40, A34 and M4 & M40 motorways. The vibrant and rapidly expanding Town has a wide range of shops centred around the sympathetically designed Woolgate Centre, traditional High Street and new central pedestrian shopping mall with major shopping brands including Marks & Spencer, Debenhams and Multi-Screen 3d cinema. The Market Square opens onto the delightful Church Green with St. Mary’s Church dominating the south side. Other amenities in the Town include supermarkets, banks, two Secondary schools and numerous Primary schools, a vast array of restaurants and public houses, a very good daily bus service into Oxford and just on the outskirts of the town is Witney lakes Resorts with golf course, health club and swimming pool. There is also a central community sports centre within the town itself.
