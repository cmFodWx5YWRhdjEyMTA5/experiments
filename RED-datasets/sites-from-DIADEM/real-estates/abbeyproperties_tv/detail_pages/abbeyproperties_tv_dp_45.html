  • DESCRIPTION

    This detached family house is located in this small hamlet of Cote between the villages of Aston and Standlake. On the ground floor the house has a fitted shaker styled kitchen with cream units and a fitted Rangermaster cooker and integrated dishwasher. This area is open to the dining room which has the stairs leading to the first floor, access to the living room and views to the garden. Within both the living room and the dining room is a central chimney breast with dual aspect into both rooms with an inset multi burning stove. At the end of the house is a beneficial study/playroom which has access to the garden via a stable door. Finally on the ground floor is the useful cloakroom/Utility room. On the first floor the master bedroom has an attractive en-suite shower room with large walk in glazed shower cubical and is fully tiled. There are 3 further bedrooms all with either wardrobes or hanging rail facilities and bedroom 4 has an interesting wooden step ladder to a mezzanine floor area which could be used for storage or a child’s small play area. The family bathroom is well fitted with a white suite with an independent shower over the whirlpool spa bath. Outside all the gardens are found to the front of the property with a wide patio area adjoining the house and steps up to the main garden area which is mostly lawn with various flower beds and borders. To the side of the garden is the driveway and single timber framed carport all of which could accommodate c.4 cars and the access to this is provided by twin remotely controlled electric gates. The total garden area is enclosed by panelled fencing.

  • LOCATION

    Cote is a small hamlet located to the East of Aston. Aston is a popular and very conveniently located village some five miles south of Witney with many excellent facilities including a primary school, nursery school, garage, village store, village hall and playing field. Aston also has a beautiful nature reserve which is the biggest of the BBOWT (Berks, Bucks and Oxon Wildlife Trust). The site totals 250 hectares of wildlife rich land along the banks of the River Thames. This vast and tranquil area of fields, pastures and nationally acclaimed wildflower meadows is a vital refuge for wading birds. The nearby former market town of Bampton offers a wider variety of facilities including an HSBC bank, post office, Budgens store, primary school, health centre and a variety of Inns and Restaurants. A limited bus service runs through Aston to Witney which offers excellent shopping, schooling and recreational facilities and there is a good onward bus service to Oxford. Bampton 1½ miles, Witney 5 miles.
