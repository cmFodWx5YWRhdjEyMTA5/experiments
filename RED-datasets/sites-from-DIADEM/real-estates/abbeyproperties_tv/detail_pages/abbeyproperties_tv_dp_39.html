    • DESCRIPTION

      Turnpike Cottage is a pretty period Cotswold stone cottage, which has undergone extensive renovation by the current vendors retaining many period features. The accommodation is approached through an entrance hall, giving access to a modern cloak/utility room, both rooms have slate tiled floors with underfloor heating and oak latch doors, which are found throughout the property. Beyond the hall is a dining room with French doors to the rear garden and an exposed stone wall and fireplace. The sitting room has a large open fireplace with a wood burning stove. This room has a solid oak floor and part exposed stone wall with stairs rising to the first floor. There is an extensively fitted shaker style gallery kitchen which includes an electric Rangemaster cooker with two ovens and six rings. There is a floor mounted combination oil boiler, twin Belfast style sink, oak work tops and slate tiled floor. The kitchen also benefits from a large built-in storage cupboard and recess for a fridge/freezer. On the first floor are three good sized bedrooms and a most attractive refitted family bathroom which includes a roll top bath with claw feet, pedestal wash hand basin and toilet and a travertine tiled shower cubicle. Outside to the front of the property, is a small gravelled enclosed garden with oil storage tank. To the rear of the property is a good sized enclosed garden, which has been laid to lawn with gravelled seating areas, flower beds and borders. The garden overlooks a paddock to the rear. There are two off-road parking spaces outside of the cottage with a further piece of land slightly away from the cottage which is big enough to offer further off-road parking and could potentially provide enough space for the construction of a single garage. The cottage benefits from mains water and electricity with a shared septic tank for waste. Viewing is highly recommended as cottages in this location and to this standard are rare to market.

    • LOCATION

      The small village of Black Bourton lies 14 miles west of Oxford, 5 miles south-west of Witney, 2 miles south of Carterton and just over a mile north of Clanfield. The village lies on gently rising ground some three miles north of the river Thames. Retaining a markedly rural character the village is a mixture of old cottages constructed of coursed limestone rubble with thatched or stone-slated roofs and newer properties. The village has a Green and play park as well as The Vines Hotel, bar and restaurant. The village also has a bus stop with regular buses going North to Carterton and running south to Bampton and on to Witney.
