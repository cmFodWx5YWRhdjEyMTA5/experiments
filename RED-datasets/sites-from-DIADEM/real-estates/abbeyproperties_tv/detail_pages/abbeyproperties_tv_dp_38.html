  • DESCRIPTION

    This substantial four bedroom detached family home set at the end of a cul-de-sac within a sought after village. This 1970’s property has been substantially extended to consist of well sized rooms that include a large living/dining room, separate sitting room and four bedrooms. On entering the property through the front door there is an entrance hall and a cloak room. Through into the main living accommodation is a large open plan Living/Dining Room. This large bright room is decorated neutrally and has patio doors at the far end that gives views of the garden. Stairs to the first floor are also located in this area. There is a separate large Sitting Room, with electric fireplace. The well sized kitchen has an excellent amount of storage units that run fully around three sides of the kitchen with plenty of floor space to put a breakfast table and chairs if required. At the far end of the kitchen is the entrance to a separate Utility Room. It is from here that you gain access to the rear garden and the entrance to the Garage. The first floor accommodation consists of three good sized double bedrooms, a single bedroom and family bathroom. Externally there is driveway parking for two cars and a garage with power to it and running water. To the rear is the garden that is mainly laid to lawn with a patio area as you walk out of the property. The property is wooden double gazed throughout, with Upvc soffits and fascias. It is our opinion that this property will make an ideal home and viewing is highly recommended.

  • LOCATION

    Aston is a very popular village south of Witney with many excellent facilities including a primary school, nursery, garage, village store, post office, village hall, pottery, shop/cafe, Church and playing field. The nearby town of Bampton offers a variety of facilities including a bank, post office, Budgens store, health centre and a variety of Inns. A bus service runs through Aston to Witney Town, which has a much wider selection of shops and general amenities then onward routes to Oxford and London.
