Crawley Road, Witney, Oxfordshire, OX28   1HT (Sold STC)
Guide Price £240,000
Bedrooms: 2
Bathroom: 1
Reception room: 1


A charming Cotswold stone cottage that is equally at home on a cold wintery night with curtains drawn and the log fire burning, as it is in the summer with the leaves back on the trees and roses flourishing around the windows, currently pictured on our sales brochures. This fine example offers two bedroom accommodation with plentiful period features and modern day essentials. The extensive renovation works carried out over recent years include opening the two small reception rooms into one delightful open space with log burning stove set within exposed stone fireplace. The refitted kitchen has a tiled floor and country cottage cream units with pewter style door furniture. There are two good size bedrooms to the first floor that are light and airy with open views to the front. The property has a replacement gas fired boiler serving radiator central heating, a refitted bathroom and is offered for sale in excellent decorative order throughout. Unlike many period properties this cottage benefits from a detached double garage with additional parking to the side, a lawned front garden in excess of 100' and is all but a short walk from Witney town centre in one direction and open countryside walks in the other. The front garden is currently open to the adjoining property but if a more private arrangement would suit a timber picket fence could be erected along the boundary line as other cottages have done in this row. A delightful property in every respect with individual features for every season.

Witney is a small country town steeped in history, but with good modern amenities and a bustling community life. The Leisure centre caters for a variety of sports and there are excellent schools, including a number of church schools. The Woolgate centre and other smaller shops have been supplemented by a new central shopping precinct with major names such as Marks & Spencer, Debenhams and in addition a multi-screen cinema. Public transport is available from outlying villages and from Witney to Oxford and on to London.
