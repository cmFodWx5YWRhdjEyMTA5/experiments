    DESCRIPTION
    The Old Inn is an imposing stone built period home situated in this lovely North Oxfordshire village with delightful walks along the canal close by. The property was formerly a farm, later a village inn and in more recent years it has become a fine private residence which is now in need of some updating. It offers exciting potential for improvement. The house offers substantial accommodation featuring particularly well proportioned rooms which are arranged over three floors. There is also an adjoining barn which could be used as a coach house style annex or be incorporated into the main house to provide further accommodation. The property retains many original features including a large inglenook fireplace in the main drawing room. Outside there are beautiful well established gardens with a pretty stream running through with bridge and waterfall. The gardens provide a delightful setting for such a lovely period home.



    LOCATION
    Somerton is a pretty North Oxfordshire village surrounded by open countryside. There is a church in the village, a local village store at Steeple Aston (c.3 miles) and a wider range of shops and amenities in Deddington and of course in Bicester. Junction 10 of the M40 is just c.3 miles away and there is also a main line railway station at neighbouring Lower Heyford providing services to both Birmingham and London via Oxford.
