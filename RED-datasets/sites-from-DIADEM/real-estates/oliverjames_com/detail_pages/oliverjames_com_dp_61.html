    DESCRIPTION
    A substantial character home converted from a former village pub, beautifully refurbished to retain many original features and to offer particularly well proportioned rooms.

    There is a spacious hall, large bespoke kitchen open to lovely dining room with open fireplace, sitting room, also with fireplace, family room, cloakroom and boot/utility room. Upstairs there are four double bedrooms, an ensuite and family bathroom. There is also a detached annex converted from a stone barn, gardens and off street parking

    LOCATION
    Upper Heyford is a charming and friendly village c.12 miles North of Oxford. The village offers a wide variety of clubs and activities including a beer festival, flower show, history society, quiz nights etc etc. There is also a village church, a pub, which serves food, a village hall and playing field. There are also delightful country walks close by, particularly the walk to the canal and lock at the bottom of Mill Lane. The nearest shop/post office is at Steeple Aston c.3 miles away although the property enjoys a lovely rural position Upper Heyford is just c.3 miles from junction 10 of the M40 and is c.1 mile away from Lower Heyford railway station.
